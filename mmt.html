Amer Fort

A Majestic Hilltop Fort
Perched atop a hill and overlooking the Maota Lake, the red sandstone fort is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site and showcases a beautiful fusion of Mughal and Rajput architecture.

Hawa Mahal

A Five-Storeyed Historic Landmark
An iconic landmark of the city, the honeycomb-shaped palace features beautiful jharokhas and windows. This five-storey building has been built without a foundation and is known for its exceptional ventilation, which is also the reason behind its name, Hawa Mahal, which literally translates to Palace of the Winds. The palace was mainly constructed to enable the ladies of the royal family to see the busy streets from the palace jharokhas.

Around one hour

Suggested Time

EXPLORE

Nahargarh Fort

The majestic abode of tigers!
Perched atop a ridge in the Aravalli Hills, Nahargarh Fort serves as a striking northern backdrop to the Jaipur city. Nahargarh, which means the abode of tigers, provided a formidable defence against the enemies in the olden days. Today, the palace remains a popular spot for local picnics and a major tourist attraction. The palace features 12 identical boudoirs for the queens and a suite for the king, all connected by corridors and adorned with intricate murals. The fort looks splendid especially when illuminated at night and offers dazzling view of the city lights below.

2-3 hours

Suggested Time

EXPLORE

City Palace

The Residence of the Royal family.
A stunning blend of Mughal and Rajput architecture, the palace is a magnificent landmark of the city. It continues to be the home of the last ruling royal family who stay in a private section of the palace. The Palace is a famous tourist attraction and features well-manicured lawns, magnificent halls and rich interiors. The City Palace Complex also houses the Maharaja Sawai Man Singh II Museum showcasing a rich collection of royal costumes, delicate shawls, silk saris, and other dresses. Additionally, the Maharani's Palace in the complex, displays fascinating Rajput weaponry, some even dating back to the 15th century.

2-3 hours

Suggested Time

EXPLORE

Birla Mandir

Discover Peace at Birla Mandir
Nestled in the heart of Rajasthan's capital, Jaipur, Birla Mandir, or Laxmi Narayan Temple, is a breathtaking architectural wonder. Crafted from pure white marble, this tranquil temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and Goddess Lakshmi. Just a short distance away, the Moti Dungri Temple, sits atop a small hill, offering lovely views of the city and is known for its unique three-domed architecture.

1 to 2 hours

Suggested Time

Jantar Mantar Near Jaipur

A Popular Astronomical Observatory
A UNESCO World Heritage Site, Jantar Mantar is one of the oldest astronomical observatories in the world, drawing historians, astronomers, architects, mathematicians and experts from around the world. It is especially known for its stone sundial, which is the worlds largest. Jantar Mantar, Jaipur, features a collection of 19 astronomical instruments that enable astronomical observations with the naked eye. This astronomical observatory is maintained as a monument today and one can take guided tours here.

Best Places to Visit in Jaipur


Jaipur is commonly referred to as the "Pink City of India," and it is a heritage destination that is both unique and magnificent. Travellers from all over the world visit Jaipur, where they are showered with gracious hospitality and treated to luxurious lodgings. You can explore the charming temples, awe-inspiring palaces and incredible forts as there are plenty of places to visit in Jaipur.

Top Places to visit in Jaipur


Amer Fort

The impressive blend of Mughal and Rajput architectural styles that can be seen in the red sandstone fort has contributed to its selection as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is among the most famous places in Jaipur. The Meena monarch Alan Singh Chanda, a member of the Chanda dynasty, was responsible for the construction of both the city of Amber and the fort that now bears his name. It is also among the top 10 places to visit in Jaipur.


Hawa Mahal

Hawa Mahal is among the most awe-inspiring places in Jaipur. Under the reign of Maharaja Sawai Pratap Singh, the palace was given the nickname "The Palace of Winds" as a result of its honeycomb architecture and 953 windows designed in the jharokha style.


Nahargarh Fort

The construction of Nahargarh also referred to as the "abode of tigers," took place in 1734 under the direction of Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II. The Nahargarh Fort, which is located on the edge of the Aravalli Hills, provides a breathtaking view of Jaipur and is counted among the top places to visit in Jaipur.


City Palace

In this city palace, which was constructed by Maharaja Sawai Jai Singh II, visitors are given a look into the lavish lifestyle of the Jaipur royal family. This palace, which is among the most stunning places in Jaipur, features magnificent halls with lavish furnishings, as well as beautifully landscaped gardens.


Jal Mahal

This magnificent structure, counted among the most scenic places to visit in Jaipur, Jal Mahal was built in the centre of the Man Sagar Lake specifically for the purpose of serving as a hunting lodge for the king. Incredible vistas of the Nahargarh Hills, which are found in the surrounding area, can be enjoyed here.


Best Time to visit Jaipur


Summer

In Jaipur, the warmest part of the year begins around the middle of March and lasts all the way through June. In the summer, highs can reach 45 degrees Celsius, while lows average approximately 25 degrees Celsius.


Monsoon

The monsoon season starts in July and continues through September. July is the beginning of the monsoon season. Although the monsoon season brings some much-appreciated moisture, it does little to relieve the suffocating heat that prevails.


Winter

October marks the beginning of winter, which continues until March when the season of spring finally arrives. Temperatures as high as 22 degrees Celsius and as low as 10 degrees Celsius are to be anticipated. It is the perfect time to explore places to visit in Jaipur.


Places to visit in Jaipur with family & friends


Jantar Mantar

This observatory was built in the early 18th century by the previous monarch of Jaipur, Maharaja Jai Singh, and is today classified as a UNESCO World Heritage Site because it features magnificent astronomical instruments. Counted among the best places in Jaipur, It is home to the sundial which is considered to be the largest one made of stone.


Birla Mandir

The breathtaking Laxmi Narayan Temple, which is one of the most revered places in Jaipur, can be found at the base of Moti Dungari Hill. The B.M. Birla Foundation constructed the building in 1988 using only white marble throughout.


Moti Doongri Temple

This temple is perched on a hilltop above the bustling city below it, and it is reported to house a Ganesha statue that is 500 years old. Moreover, it provides a view of the famous Birla Mandir. In 1761, Seth Jai Ram Paliwal was in charge of supervising the building's construction. You must include this site in your itinerary of places to visit in Jaipur in 2 days.


Best Places Jaipur for couples


Sisodia Rani ka Bagh

The Sisodia Rani Garden and Palace can be found in the metropolitan area of Jaipur, which is found in the state of Rajasthan in India. Each of the multiple garden terraces, which are layered on one another, has its own unique collection of water features, winding walks, and brightly coloured pavilions.


Rambagh Palace

The former residence of the Maharaja of Jaipur was the Rambagh Palace, which is located on Bhawani Singh road about 8 km outside of the city walls. It is among the best Jaipur places to visit in 3 days


Central Park

If you are looking for Jaipur places to visit in 1 day, then you can take your partner to Central Park where you can simply lounge around.


Good Places to visit at Night in Jaipur


Blackout Club Jaipur

This private rooftop club serves upscale meals, hosts dance parties featuring guest DJs, and offers breathtaking vistas of the city below. If you enjoy good vibes and drinks, then it is among the best places to visit in Jaipur.


WTF Sports Cafe & Bar

Asteria

This is a terrace bar that specialises in North Indian, Italian, and Mexican cuisine, as well as offering a wide selection of alcoholic beverages and screening live sports events.


Best places to visit near Jaipur


Alwar

Alwar is an incredibly underrated city in Rajasthan and is among the most stunning nearby places to visit in Jaipur. It is home to the Alwar City Palace was constructed in 1793 and contains a garden that is filled with marble pavilions that are set on bases fashioned like lotuses. You can also find a large number of temples here.


Ajmer

The city of Ajmer is located in the state of Rajasthan. Ajmer is known for its religious shrines as well as insightful museums and bustling markets.


Pushkar

The city of Pushkar is home to a significant number of places of worship and is among the most popular near by places to visit in Jaipur. Not to mention, Pushkar is also known to be a popular destination among travellers, especially during the Holi festival.
